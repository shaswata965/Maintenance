Lesson 20
The 7 Keys to Successful Diabetes Management

Diabetes is a lifelong condition, but you can still live a full and happy life by just continuing to practice the healthy habits you have worked so hard to build.

This is the great time to think about what you can do NOW that you couldn’t do when you started the program.

  • Healthy Eating

    Having diabetes doesn’t mean you have to follow a strict diet. It’s just about portion control and understanding how different foods affect your blood sugar levels.


  • Being Active

    Staying active throughout the day has endless benefits. It improves mood and memory, increases strength, reduces stress… With diabetes, a brisk walk alone can help control blood sugar levels.


  • Monitoring

    The only way to know if your blood sugar levels are in the target range is by checking your blood sugar levels regularly. It gives you vital information about how you’re doing and if you need to adjust your food and activity to stay in range.


  • Taking Medication

    Everyone has their own individualized medication plan. Taking your medication on time will help lower your blood sugar levels and reduce your risk for future complications.


  • Problem Solving

    Having the right problem-solving skills can help you prepare for the unexpected and know how to overcome challenges you may face on a regular basis.


  • Reducing Risks

    Diabetes places you at higher risk for other diseases like high blood pressure and high cholesterol. Good news is that there are steps you can take to reduce your risk for these diseases!


  • Healthy Coping

    Diabetes may affect you physically, but it also can take an emotional toll on you and your identity. What’s important is to know how to deal with these emotions in a healthy way to prevent them from negatively affecting your diabetes self-care.
