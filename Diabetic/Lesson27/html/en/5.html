Lesson 27
How to Prepare for Emergencies

If your blood sugar gets so low you have trouble eating or drinking, you may pass out and have a seizure.

That’s why it’s important for people close to you to know how to administer a glucagon injection. Glucagon is a hormone that signals to your body to release sugar into the blood quickly.

Tell friends, family members, and coworkers to give you glucagon — not insulin — if you can’t swallow the carbs you need, you pass out, or you have a seizure.

Make sure they know to call emergency care right after they give you glucagon.

After you get the glucagon and you can swallow again, start taking small sips of juice or full-sugar soda. Then eat a full meal.
