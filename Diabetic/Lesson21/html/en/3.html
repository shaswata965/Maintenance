Lesson 21
While You’re Traveling
  • If you’re driving, pack a cooler with healthy foods and plenty of water to drink.
  • Don’t store insulin or diabetes medicine in direct sunlight or in a hot car; keep them in the cooler too. Don’t put insulin directly on ice or a gel pack.
  • Heat can also damage your blood sugar monitor, test strips, insulin pump, and other diabetes equipment. Don’t leave them in a hot car, by a pool, in direct sunlight, or on the beach.
  • You can always find healthy food options at the airport or a roadside restaurant: Fruit, nuts, sandwiches, yogurt, Salads with chicken or fish, Eggs, Burgers with a lettuce wrap instead of a bun
  • If you’re in a plane or car, stop and get out of the car or walk up and down the aisle of the plane every hour or two to prevent blood clots (people with diabetes are at higher risk).
  • Set an alarm for taking medicine if you’re traveling across time zones.
