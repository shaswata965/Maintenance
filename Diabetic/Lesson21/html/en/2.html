Before Leaving

Check in with your doctor.

Let them know your planned activities and find out how it can affect your diabetes and what to do about it.

Get a prescription for your medication in case you run out

Find the nearest pharmacies to where you will be staying during the trip

Get a medical identification card or bracelet that states you have diabetes

Consider travel insurance just in case you need medical care

Pack your own meal for the trip that meets your own needs

When it comes to packing,

Place your diabetes supplies in a carry-on bag (insulin could get too cold in your checked luggage). Think about bringing a smaller bag to have at your seat for insulin, glucose tablets, and snacks.

Pack twice as much medicine as you think you’ll need. Carry medicines in the pharmacy bottles they came in, or ask your pharmacist to print out extra labels you can attach to plastic bags.

Be sure to pack healthy snacks, like fruit, raw veggies, and nuts.

Good news: people with diabetes are exempt from the 3.4 oz. liquid rule for medicines, fast-acting carbs like juice, and gel packs to keep insulin cool.

Very important:

If you use a continuous glucose monitor or insulin pump, do not run them through the X-ray machine. They can get damaged. You don’t have to disconnect from either; ask for a hand inspection instead.
