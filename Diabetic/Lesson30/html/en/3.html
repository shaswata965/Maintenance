How does diabetes affect my cholesterol?

Your body uses blood sugar for energy and insulin is what carries it to muscles. People with diabetes do not have enough insulin to move blood glucose from their blood to muscles. The liver works hard to fix this by combining fat (triglycerides) and cholesterol for use as an energy source for muscles.

Cholesterol is a fat-like substance produced by your liver that helps your body make vitamin D and some hormones. Cholesterol that comes from the food you eat is referred to as “dietary cholesterol”.

You can find cholesterol in many animal products, like:

Red meat

Eggs

Butter

Fish
