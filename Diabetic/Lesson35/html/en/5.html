Diabetic-Friendly Desserts

Do you plan on having dessert this week? Try out our suggestions for low-carb desserts that wonâ€™t put a strain on your diabetes.

  • 1. Fruit

    Fruits are one the best desserts you can have! They contain natural sugars with fiber so they can satisfy your sweet tooth and slow down the absorption of sugar. Enjoy a fruit parfait with low-fat yogurt!

  • 2. Dark Chocolate

    You may have already heard that eating chocolate can good for your health. The truth of the matter is more research needs to be done to give recommendations about chocolate and its effect on health.

    It turns out chocolate has the potential to provide your body with health benefits. Certain types of chocolates contain high levels of flavonoids, which are antioxidants that help the body repair damaged cells, which can reduce the risk of heart disease and cancer.

    Here are some tips to choose the highest-quality, least processed and most nutritious bars of chocolate:

    • Choose dark chocolate that contains at least 60% cocoa
    • Keep your portions small (thumbs size) ALWAYS!

Droobi Tips..

Avoid chocolate with candy coatings, creamy centers or added nuts; these pack extra calories and sugar!

  • 3. Homemade desserts

    When you prepare homemade cake or cookies, you can control the ingredients and its quality. Aim for whole wheat flour in the cake, use unsweetened apple sauce, less sugar and fat. Cut it in small portions, leave one individual portion on the table and freeze the rest!

  • 4. Frozen desserts

    When it comes to ice cream and frozen yogurt, both are very carb-rich options. If you are ordering regular ice cream and yogurt, choose a mini or kid-size cup.

    You can also try to make your own sugar-free frozen desserts. Blend any cup of frozen berries and add a splash of orange juice for a homemade sorbet!
