Lesson 23
The Relapse Chain

Let’s visit Laila’s story to get a better idea of how a lapse can lead to a total relapse:

  1. Leila enters a high-risk situation. She attends a wedding late at night. All Leila ate that day was a small snack. When she arrives, Leila is very hungry to wait for the main entrees and ends up indulging in the salted nuts, chocolates, cakes, and other sweets being offered.
  2. Leila has no plan for how to cope. Leila did not bring a healthy snack with her, so she cannot satisfy her hunger with something healthy and is left with only unhealthy options
  3. Slip-up happens. Leila begins to indulge in the chocolates offered to her
  4. Negative thinking takes over and there is no plan to move forward. Leila comes home from the wedding feeling enormous guilt. She feels like she has ruined all the progress she has made with this one meal.
  5. Another slip-up happens and there is no plan for come back. Leila goes to work the next day and finds everyone snacking on cupcakes that one of her colleagues handmade.
  6. Leila sleeps late and wakes up late, has to rush to get her children to school. She misses breakfast and stops by for coffee. She sees other pastries that look tempting, so she orders a chocolate croissant.
  7. Full relapse. Leila has continued to eat unhealthily, thinking that she will eventually stop, but still continues. A few weeks later, she is 5 kilo heavier.

Here’s the good news: everyone slips up and it is completely normal. Slipping up every once in a while, will not affect your progress to achieving your goals. What will affect your progress is how you react to slipping up.
