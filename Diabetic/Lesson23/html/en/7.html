Lesson 23
Plan ahead

Planning ahead for these high-risk situations can help you deal with it without spiraling into bad habits. Make a plan. Write it down. Your plan should involve actions to change the situation, your thoughts and behavior or both. The more specific it will be, the more likely you can follow it!

While you’re planning for your comeback after a small lapse, keep these things in mind:

  1. One mistake does not discount all the progress you made in the past.
  2. You’re trying to change for a reason - remember your purpose.
  3. Talk to yourself like you would talk to your friend, in a kind manner. Think of this as a learning experience that will make you better equipped for the future.

It’s important to realize that weight loss is not always about the decisions you choose to make. It has everything to do with how your body responds to different factors.

Living a healthy lifestyle isn’t about “successes” and “failures”. Losing weight and keeping it off are part of cycle that needs you to continue living a well-balanced life of practicing healthy habits.
