How Heat Affects Blood Sugar Levels

Extreme heat can affect blood sugar control in people with diabetes.

When you sweat more, you are losing water, becoming more dehydrated, resulting in a higher concentration of sugar in your body. High blood sugar can make you urinate more, and cause dehydration, fueling the cycle.

Also, high temperatures can change how your body uses insulin. You might need to check your blood sugar more often and ask your doctor about modifying your medication.

Sweating is the main process everybody uses to cool down. Diabetes-related complications affect the body’s sweat glands, which can interfere with its ability to cool itself. This is especially true if it’s humid outside.

If you live in a hot area, it’s a good idea to be familiar with the heat index- a measurement that combines temperature and humidity. If you’re outside and it reaches 80°F in the shade with 40% humidity or above, it’s important for your health to take the proper steps to cool down. Staying physically active improves your ability to manage your blood sugar, but don’t get active outdoors during the hottest part of the day or when the heat index is high. Get out early in the morning or in the evening when temperatures are lower, or go to an air-conditioned mall or gym to get active.
