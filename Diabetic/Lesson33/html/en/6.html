Lesson 33
Check the Food Label

Do your best to not fall for the food label lies! “Sugar free” does not mean healthy! Read the nutrition label to understand what you are considering of putting into your body.

  • Carbohydrates

    Check the “total carbohydrates” which will affect your blood sugar. Everyone has a different target for total carbs for the day. Compare the recommended serving size found on the box (2 slices) with how much you were planning to eat (4 slices). A good rule of thumb is to have 1-2 servings per meal or snack.

  • Fiber

    Always choose the options that are marked as “whole-grain” or “whole-wheat”. Good sources of fiber have 2 grams or more of fiber. The best sources of fiber are beans, vegetables and fruits!

  • Fats

    Heart-healthy fats in foods, like olive oil, avocado, and nuts is what you should look for! Always check your portions since even healthy fats are still high in calories. Avoid foods high in saturated fat and containing trans-fat.
