Handy Guide for Portions

Most people tend to eat almost all of what they serve themselves. This is why controlling portion sizes can help prevent overeating.

Here’s a handy guide you can use to eat the correct size portion of different foods.

Fist = 1 cup Fresh leafy greens
Yogurt
Half fist = ½ cup Cooked beans and lentils
Cupped hand = ¼ cup Nuts
Dried fruit
Seeds
Palm = 2.5 oz 1 portion of protein foods like chicken, fish, beef and lamb
Flat hand 1 slice of toast or ½ a bagel, pita or tortilla
Thumb = 1 tablespoon Oil and spreads like peanut butter and sauces
Cheese (2 thumbs)
Thumb tip = 1 tsp Salt

It’s up to you how you want to measure your food – cups and spoons. An easy way to see whether you’re eating too much is to compare the amount to your hands and thumbs.
