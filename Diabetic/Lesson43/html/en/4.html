Lesson 43
How to Fast Safely

Monitor your Blood Sugar

  • Pricking your finger does NOT break your fast
  • Checking your blood sugar will let you know when your blood sugar levels are too high or low
  • Best times to check is in the morning, midday, before breaking fast and after

Have Balanced Meals

  • Make sure your whole plate is not filled with starches when you break your fast
  • It’s best to have a combination of starches, vegetables, lean meats and healthy fats
  • Make sure to eat your pre-dawn meal (suhur) to help control your blood sugar levels during the day

Stay Hydrated

  • During non-fasting hours, make sure to drink enough water and limit the sugary drinks and fruit juices

Keep Moving

  • Exercise can be continued during Ramadan but try to avoid high intensity exercises before the fast starts.
  • Even the nightly prayers can be considered as exercise

Break your fast if…

  • Blood sugars are less than 70 mg/dl
  • Blood sugars are less than 90 mg/dl in the first few hours of the fast, especially after medication
  • Blood sugar levels are above 300 mg/dl
  • Feeling unwell or sick

Don’t skip these key points if you plan to fast during Ramadan. Always reach out to your doctor for more information about fasting during Ramadan with diabetes.
