Tips to Keep Your Feet Healthy

There are also many things you can do to keep your feet healthy.

  • Take care of your diabetes

    Work with your health care team to maintain in range blood sugar levels

  • Check your feet every day

    Look at your bare feet for red spots, cuts, swelling, and blisters. If you cannot see the bottoms of your feet, use a mirror or ask someone for help.


  • Stay active

    Share your plan for physical activity with your doctor and see how it might affect you.


  • Keep your feet clean

    Wash your feet every day. Dry them carefully, especially between the toes.


  • Keep your skin soft and smooth

    Rub a thin coat of skin lotion over the tops and bottoms of your feet, but not between your toes.


  • If you can see and reach your toenails, trim them

    Do this when needed, always straight across and file the edges with an emery board or nail file.


  • Never walk barefoot

    Wear shoes and socks at all times. Wear comfortable shoes that fit well and protect your feet. Check inside your shoes before wearing them. Make sure the lining is smooth and there are no objects inside.


  • Protect your feet from hot and cold

    Wear shoes at the beach or on hot pavement. Don't put your feet into hot water. Test the temperature of any water you plan on putting your feet. Avoid using hot water bottles, heating pads, or electric blankets. You can burn your feet without realizing it.


  • Keep the blood flowing to your feet

    Put your feet up when sitting. Wiggle your toes and move your ankles up and down for 5 minutes, two or three times a day. Don't cross your legs for long periods of time.


  • Take action now

    Set a time every day to check your feet.
