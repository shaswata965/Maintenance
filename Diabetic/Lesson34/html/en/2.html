Lesson 34
The 7 Diabetes-Related Complications

There is greater awareness and better control of the risk factors and behaviors that can lead to complications. Different complications share the same risk factors, and one complication can make another complication worse. For example, people with diabetes are at higher risk for high blood pressure. High blood pressure can worsen eye and kidney disease, which are complications from diabetes.

Let’s take a look at what are the major diabetes complications:

  • Heart disease and stroke

    People with diabetes are twice as likely to have heart disease or stroke than people without diabetes.

  • Eye disease

    Did you know that diabetic retinopathy, a form of eye disease, is one of the most preventable causes of vision loss? There are three different types of eye disease related to diabetes. All of them can lead to impaired vision.

    • Diabetic retinopathy: damage to blood vessels
    • Cataract: clouding of lens
    • Glaucoma: increase in fluid pressure in eye

  • Nerve damage (neuropathy)

    Nerve damage can cause numbness and pain in feet. But it can also affect digestion, blood vessels and heart.

  • Kidney Disease

    High blood sugar levels can cause kidney damage, which can eventually lead to chronic kidney disease. If not treated, it can lead to kidney failure, which requires dialysis or a kidney transplant.

  • Amputations

    Mismanaged diabetes can cause damage to blood vessels and nerves, especially in the feet, which can lead to life-threatening infections. An amputation might be necessary to stop the spread of infection.

  • Gum disease

    It’s important to take good care of your teeth when you have diabetes. Did you know that people with diabetes are more likely to have problems with their teeth and gums? There are steps you can take to keep your teeth healthy, like

    • Getting a dental exam every year,
    • Checking your mouth for red and swollen gums, loose teeth, bad breath
    • Flossing once a day
    • Brushing with a soft toothbrush at least twice a day
    • Eating a well-balanced diet and limiting foods and drinks high in sugar


  • Mental Health Disorders

    The risk for developing mental health disorders like depression grows when living with diabetes.
