Two Types of Fiber

Soluble fiber is the type of fiber that slows the absorption of sugar into your blood. It can be found in foods like oatmeal, beans and lentils, eggplant and okra.

Insoluble fiber is a type of fiber that does not blend with water, unlike soluble fiber. This type of fiber acts as a bulking agent for digestion. You can find insoluble fiber in foods like whole-wheat breads, brown rice, couscous and wheat bran.

Droobi Tip

When you eat fiber-rich foods, drinking water can help you gain the most benefits of fiber and avoid bloating.
