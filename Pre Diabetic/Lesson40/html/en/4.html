How to Think Positive

While thinking positive alone will not make you lose weight and solve your problems, it can certainly help you stay on track and make the overall process easier.


  • Focus on what’s going right

    Even when bad things happen, you can still open your mind to find the good.


  • Practice gratitude

    This goes hand in hand with focusing on what’s right. Write 3 things that you’re grateful for that bring you a sense of comfort, joy or happiness. This will prime your brain to always find the good, even if things go wrong.


  • Keep a sense of humor

    Without a doubt, having a good laugh can lift your mood. It’s even better when it’s with friends!


  • Get support

    Sometimes, another person can help put things into perspective. Being an outsider allows someone to see a “bad” situation in its whole. A close friend, family member, or colleague can advise you how to cope with stress.


  • Practice positive self-talk

    Did you accidentally eat too much while out with your friends and now you feel guilty? Talk to yourself like you would talk with a friend. You would never tell a friend that they should feel guilty for making a mistake. Use each set-back as an opportunity to learn more about your strengths and weaknesses (we all have them!).
