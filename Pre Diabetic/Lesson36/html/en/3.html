The Benefits of Fasting

Studies have shown that practicing intermittent fasting:

  • Supports weight loss. When you restrict the window of time you spend eating, it’s easier to eat fewer calories.
  • Maintains muscle. There is some evidence that fasting can help you hold on to more muscle mass when trying to lose weight.
  • Reduces risk for type 2 diabetes. Also, when you’re not eating, your levels of insulin are lowered, which improves your insulin resistance, ultimately lowering your risk for diabetes.
  • Reduces body inflammation. Studies show that fasting fights against inflammation in the body, which is beneficial for many diseases and the aging process.
  • Prevents cancer. Fasting can help prevent different cancers because of its effect on your body’s cells.
  • Promotes heart health. Fasting has been shown to improve blood pressure, cholesterol levels and triglycerides.
  • Protects brain health. Fasting can protect against brain damage, prevent Alzheimer’s disease and promote the growth of new neurons.
  • Increases life span. Some studies have shown that it can increase your lifespan, which shouldn’t come as a surprise given all the benefits for your whole body and health!
