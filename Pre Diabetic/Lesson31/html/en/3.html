The Best of the Worst

Whenever youâ€™re choosing a meal, follow these guidelines to narrow down your choices:

Choose meals that are high in:

  • Fiber: vegetables, fruits, legumes and whole-grains, will keep you full and prevent you from overeating
  • Non-starchy vegetables: are filled with vitamins and minerals that will help keep your body healthy

Avoid meals that are high in:

  • Fat: while avocados and olive oil are heathy sources of fat, they are high in calories. Enjoy it in small portions. Fast foods and baked goods are loaded with calories and unhealthy fats that increase your risk of heart disease.
  • Sodium: a high salt diet is one of the main causes of high blood pressure. Packaged and processed foods like canned soup, store-bought salad dressing, and other canned foods are high in sodium. Always check the food label!
  • Sugar: many of the items in the grocery store have some form of added sugar. Added sugar products will keep you hungry, leading to bigger consumption of calories.
