Feeling Alert at Night

When we’re worried about something, it can keep us up all night thinking about it! Feeling anxious or alert at night is something that happens to all of us. It can be a serious sleep disorder if it happens all the time.

Here are some ways to cope if you can’t seem to fall asleep:

Check your lifestyle. Make sure you’re eating well and moving enough during the day. Food and exercise play a big role in helping you unwind and sleep at night

Avoid caffeine

Avoid naps

Manage your stress levels with relaxation techniques, like deep breathing and meditation

If you smoke, start the process to quit

Check with your doctor if any medications you are taking are keeping you up at night

When it’s close to your bedtime:

Avoid looking at screens (computer, phone, TV). The light can make your brain think it’s time to be awake

Avoid working or being active

Stick to a bedtime routine you can follow everyday. This will give a signal to your brain that it’s time to fall asleep.

At bedtime:

Try to sleep at the same time each day. Your body will get on a schedule

Don’t pressure yourself to fall asleep. Don’t worry about time passing

If you think you won’t be able to sleep soon, don’t stay in bed. Get up and do something relaxing like reading.
