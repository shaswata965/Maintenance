Lesson 47
How to Deal with a Sugar Craving

Cravings are a signal from your brain telling you that you need some form of “reward” and sugar is something that your body recognizes as a reward.

Cravings are not a signal that your body needs food or water!

To combat cravings, try the following:

  1. Eat a well-balanced and filling meal

    Cravings are not the same as hunger. But if you are hungry, you can also develop a craving. Being hungry while having a craving can be very difficult to control. Cravings are temporary, real hunger will need food.

    If you find yourself hungry and having a sugar craving, have a full, well-balanced meal that includes vegetables and lean protein. It might not be enjoyable at first, because your body is telling you that you need sugar to be happy, but satisfying your hunger will stop the craving for good. Protein rich foods like meat, fish and eggs are known for reducing hunger!

  2. Distract yourself with a walk

    Doing some light exercise like going for a walk can help keep your mind off your craving and release chemicals in your brain that make you feel good, which also helps to turn off a craving. If you can’t go outside for a walk, any form of light exercise like stairs or lifting dumbbells will work.
