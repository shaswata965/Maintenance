More Tips to Reduce Cravings

To prevent cravings from happening in the first place, stick to your regular healthy meals and exercise plan and toss out any junk food in the house!

If the 3-step plan didn’t help, here are other ways you can deal with a craving:

  1. Drink water

    It can help you feel “fuller” and satisfied.

  2. Have a fruit serving

    The natural sugar in a fruit will satisfy your sugar craving without affecting your weight progress.

  3. Eat more protein

    Protein rich foods will help keep you full and prevent cravings from happening in the first place!

  4. Chat with a friend

    This is a great way to distract yourself and get more support!

  5. Sleep well

    Studies show that not getting enough sleep can cause you to crave and eat more the next day.

  6. Manage stress

    Stress can make your brain crave a “reward” like sugar. Relaxation techniques like deep breathing can keep your stress levels in check.

  7. Control triggers around you

    Try to avoid unhealthy triggers like nearby fast-food chains and unhealthy junk food.

  8. Remind yourself of your goals

    Think about what you want to achieve and why not giving into this craving is worth it!

  9. Avoid skipping meals!

    Skipping meals can cause you to be extremely hungry later in the day, leaving room for cravings to come in!

  10. Check with your doctor

    If you regularly have very strong cravings, check with your doctor to see if you have any vitamin deficiencies that might be causing them.
