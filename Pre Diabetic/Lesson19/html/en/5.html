Lesson 19
Fiber

Fiber is a type of carbohydrate that passes through the body without being digested. It’s found only in plant foods like vegetables, fruits, whole grains, and lentils.

  • High-fiber foods, like fruits and vegetables, tend to be low in fat and calories and high in vitamins and minerals. Fiber also helps in lowering cholesterol levels.


  • Many high-fiber foods take longer to chew and therefore allow time for your body to realize that you are full.


  • Like a sponge, fiber can absorb water. This adds “bulk” to your intestines, which makes you feel full, preventing you from overeating, and promotes healthy digestion.
