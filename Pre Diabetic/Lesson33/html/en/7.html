Other Strategies to Stay Motivated

Pick what works for you

Don’t set goals for activity that you don’t enjoy. Having a healthy lifestyle means practicing healthy habits and behaviors because you want to enjoy them!

Track your progress

Keep tracking your food and activity, this will help you understand how far or close you are from your goals.

Get support

Talking with a friend about your progress can give you a boost when you feel down. It’s a bonus if someone close to you is also working on having a healthier lifestyle.

Celebrate your wins

Reached your weight loss goal for the week? Plan a fun evening with your friends. Just make sure your reward does not involve food. Food rewards can set you back.

Think positive

People who are hopeful after a setback are more likely to bounce back stronger. Thinking negatively after a mistake can make you want to do it again out of guilt. Stop the cycle by thinking positively and feeling hopeful about your situation. Remember, we all make mistakes!

Drop perfectionism

Your path will not be perfect. Wanting everything you eat and do to be perfect is not realistic. Life happens and sometimes plans change. When you make a mistake, talk to yourself like you would talk to your friend – in an encouraging and empathetic way!

Have a positive body image

Studies show that people who dislike the shape of their bodies are less likely to lose weight.
